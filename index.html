Phase 3 · Now Active
Deep Engagement & Barrier Visibility
Sustained engagement is surfacing barriers that standard outreach doesn't identify — social determinants, behavioral health needs, and care access gaps that exist in the quiet moments between appointments. This is why members aren't connecting to care.
What this data tells us "Are we learning anything meaningful that can change outcomes?" Yes. 554 members have completed SDOH screenings — 96–100% across all 4 markets identify at least one social need. Economic instability is the primary barrier in every market. PHQ-4 and UCLA-3 baselines are now established, creating the measurement foundation for demonstrating clinical change over time. This is the data that drives everything downstream.
What We're Seeing by Market
What comes next: Once barriers are identified, we remove them. PCP scheduling, SDOH resource connections, AWV support, and reward redemption tracking activate in Phase 4. Those outcomes appear below.
