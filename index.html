Launch & Operational Readiness

Phase 2 · Ongoing
Reach & Response
Outreach continues across all 4 markets — new members are enrolling every week. Reach & Response runs in parallel with Deep Engagement: members who enrolled in Month 1 are already in barrier work while new members are still being brought in.
What this data tells us "Are we reaching the right people?" Members are responding. 20.88% of members reached are enrolling — more than double the 10% contractual minimum. Ohio leads at 21.7%, with Texas, Utah, and Washington all performing at or above 20%. Every enrolled member moves into needs-based conversation with a dedicated care team member within the first engagement.
Talking Points — Reach & Response
Conversion rate — "DSNP members are among the most complex and hardest-to-reach populations in managed care. The fact that nearly 1 in 5 members reached are choosing to engage — and stay engaged — is a strong early signal that this population wants this support. They just haven't had access to it."
Ohio at 21.7%, all 4 markets above 20% — "Washington launched weeks later than the other markets, serves the population Molina specifically flagged as the hardest to reach, and has now enrolled 1,033 members — the highest volume of any market. Ohio leads at 21.7%. The barriers are real — but members are responding at every market, with every cohort."

Phase 3 · Now Active
Deep Engagement & Barrier Visibility
Sustained engagement is surfacing barriers that standard outreach doesn't identify — social determinants, behavioral health needs, and care access gaps that exist in the quiet moments between appointments. This is why members aren't connecting to care.
What this data tells us "Are we learning anything meaningful that can change outcomes?" Yes. 554 members have completed SDOH screenings — 96–100% across all 4 markets identify at least one social need. Economic instability is the primary barrier in every market. PHQ-4 and UCLA-3 baselines are now established, creating the measurement foundation for demonstrating clinical change over time. This is the data that drives everything downstream.
Talking Points — Deep Engagement & Barrier Visibility
SDOH screening completion (~95% in TX, UT, WA) — "We typically see around 70% completion on SDOH assessments. We're running at 95% across three of your four markets. That reflects two things: genuine trust between your members and our care team, and the fact that these members want to talk about what's getting in their way. You can't solve barriers you can't see — now we can see them, at scale."
PHQ-4 baselines established — "We've documented mental health baselines across hundreds of members. This creates the measurement foundation to demonstrate improvement in HOS emotional health outcomes over time. You can't move a measure you haven't first established."
UCLA-3 loneliness baselines — "Social isolation is one of the least visible drivers of low member experience scores — and one of the hardest things for a plan to measure on its own. We now have baseline loneliness scores for enrolled members. Consistent engagement directly addresses this."
Avg 3.0 social needs per member — "Members are carrying an average of three concurrent social needs. These aren't isolated problems — they're layered barriers that require coordinated navigation, not a single referral. That's exactly what our care team is built to do."

Molina Priority 1
Establish PCP Relationships
"Help DSNP members establish a relationship with a Primary Care Provider."
PCP connections are now activating. 9 visits confirmed across TX, WA, and UT — including 3 direct PCP connections, 1 Annual Wellness Exam, and preventive care visits supporting STARS quality measures. Economic instability, housing insecurity, and transportation gaps remain the primary barriers in every market, and 2,687 care team conversations are the mechanism removing them.
Value Journey Transitioning from Deep Engagement to Activation — PCP connections and preventive visits confirmed in TX, WA, and UT. SDOH resolution tracking and AWV attestation in progress.
Talking Points — PCP Access
6,588 care team conversations — "These aren't automated reminders or text blasts. These are needs-based conversations with a dedicated care team member — the kind of interaction that builds the trust required to get a DSNP member to schedule and keep a PCP appointment."
9 visits confirmed across TX, WA & UT — "PCP connections are happening — 3 markets have confirmed visits, including an Annual Wellness Exam in WA that is a direct STARS attestation opportunity. OH activation is the next priority."
Molina Priority 2
Improve Member Experience
"Improve member experience — benefits navigation, health needs, and access to the right resources."
Member experience is driven by whether members feel heard, supported, and able to navigate their care. We've now established loneliness and distress baselines across the enrolled population — the metrics that most directly predict CAHPS and HOS scores. These baselines give us the ability to demonstrate change over time, not just activity.
Value Journey Baselines established in Deep Engagement. CAHPS/HOS movement is a 12–18 month lagging indicator — the measurement foundation is being built now.
Talking Points — Member Experience
UCLA-3 loneliness baselines — "Social isolation is one of the least visible drivers of low CAHPS scores — and one of the hardest things for a plan to measure. We now have loneliness baselines for every enrolled member. Consistent engagement with the same care team member directly addresses this. Consistent with published research on social isolation and patient experience outcomes."
PHQ-4 distress baselines — "Distressed members navigate care less effectively and report lower satisfaction even when care quality is equivalent. We've established mental health baselines that create the foundation to demonstrate HOS emotional health improvement over time. You can't move a measure you haven't first established."
3,218 UCLA-3 and 3,190 PHQ-4 screenings completed — "We have loneliness and distress baselines on the enrolled population — the metrics that most directly predict CAHPS and HOS scores. Members average 4.5–4.8 on loneliness and 2.6–3.2 on distress across markets. That's the measurement foundation. You can't move a measure you haven't first established."
Molina Priority 3
Support Quality Improvement
"Support quality improvement in underperforming markets by engaging higher-risk members."
SDOH screening completion is the direct precursor to closing gaps in Staying Healthy measures — the domain where Molina's DSNP markets have the most room to improve. Members with unaddressed social needs skip preventive screenings. Identifying and removing those barriers is what turns engagement into quality movement.
Value Journey Transitioning from Deep Engagement to Activation — 3 preventive care attestations confirmed across TX and WA, including 1 Cancer Screening (Mammogram) and 1 AWV directly supporting STARS quality measures.
Talking Points — Quality Improvement
SDOH screening as quality leading indicator — "Every SDOH screening is a data point that didn't exist before. Members with identified barriers are the same members who skip AWVs and preventive screenings. Now that we can see the barrier, we can remove it — and when the barrier is removed, the screening happens. That's how this moves Staying Healthy measures."
3 preventive care attestations confirmed — "Star rating movement is a 12–18 month lagging indicator. But we're already seeing the early outputs in Month 3 — preventive visits, an AWV, and a Cancer Screening directly in the Staying Healthy STARS domain. The leading indicators are converting to real outcomes."
